Variation of Parameters

An advanced web platform designed to analyze, solve, and visualize second-order non-homogeneous linear ordinary differential equations.

Aim

To study, analyze, and analytically solve non-homogeneous linear second-order ordinary differential equations of the type $y'' + y = X(x)$ by implementing the method of **Variation of Parameters**.

General Theory & Foundations

The **Method of Variation of Parameters** is a powerful analytical technique used to find a particular solution $y_p(x)$ of a non-homogeneous linear second-order ordinary differential equation. Unlike the method of undetermined coefficients, it does not restrict the forcing function $X(x)$ to polynomials, exponentials, or sinusoids.

1. The Standard Form & Complementary Function

Consider the linear second-order non-homogeneous differential equation with constant coefficients:

$$y'' + P(x)y' + Q(x)y = X(x)$$

First, we find the complementary solution $y_c(x)$ of the corresponding homogeneous equation ($X(x) = 0$), expressed as a linear combination of two independent base solutions $y_1(x)$ and $y_2(x)$:

$$y_c(x) = c_1 y_1(x) + c_2 y_2(x)$$

2. The Wronskian Determinant

To ensure that $y_1(x)$ and $y_2(x)$ form a valid fundamental set of solutions, they must be **linearly independent**. This is verified using the **Wronskian ($W$)**, which is the determinant of the matrix formed by the functions and their first derivatives:

$$\displaystyle W(y_1, y_2) = \begin{vmatrix} y_1(x) & y_2(x) \\ y_1'(x) & y_2'(x) \end{vmatrix} = y_1(x)y_2'(x) - y_2(x)y_1'(x)$$

Theorem: If the solutions $y_1$ and $y_2$ are derived from a homogeneous linear ODE, their Wronskian is either identically zero everywhere or never zero on a given interval. If $W \neq 0$, the solutions are linearly independent.

3. Varying the Parameters

We replace the constants $c_1$ and $c_2$ with variable parameters $u(x)$ and $v(x)$ to construct the particular solution:

$$y_p(x) = u(x)y_1(x) + v(x)y_2(x)$$

By applying the condition $u'y_1 + v'y_2 = 0$ during differentiation, we isolate expressions for the parameter derivatives, which yield the integration formulas:

$$\displaystyle u(x) = \int \frac{-y_2(x) X(x)}{W} \, dx \qquad \text{and} \qquad v(x) = \int \frac{y_1(x) X(x)}{W} \, dx$$

Solved Example (Step-by-Step)

Problem Statement: Find the general solution of $\quad y'' + y = \sec(x)$

Step 1: Find the Complementary Function ($y_c$)

The characteristic auxiliary equation for the homogeneous part $y'' + y = 0$ is:

$$m^2 + 1 = 0 \implies m = \pm i$$

This gives us the fundamental independent solution set:

$$y_1 = \cos(x), \quad y_2 = \sin(x)$|$$
$$y_c(x) = c_1\cos(x) + c_2\sin(x)$$

Step 2: Calculate the Wronskian ($W$)

Compute the determinant of the fundamental solution set matrix:

$$\displaystyle W = \begin{vmatrix} \cos(x) & \sin(x) \\ -\sin(x) & \cos(x) \end{vmatrix} = (\cos(x)\cdot\cos(x)) - (\sin(x)\cdot(-\sin(x)))$$ $$W = \cos^2(x) + \sin^2(x) = 1$$

Since $W = 1 \neq 0$, the solutions are linearly independent configurations.

Step 3: Integrate the Parameters $u(x)$ and $v(x)$

Using the integration rules with $X(x) = \sec(x)$ and $W = 1$:

$$\displaystyle u(x) = \int \frac{-y_2 X(x)}{W}\,dx = \int \frac{-\sin(x)\cdot\sec(x)}{1}\,dx = \int -\tan(x)\,dx = \ln|\cos(x)|$$
$$\displaystyle v(x) = \int \frac{y_1 X(x)}{W}\,dx = \int \frac{\cos(x)\cdot\sec(x)}{1}\,dx = \int \cos(x)\cdot\frac{1}{\cos(x)}\,dx = \int 1\,dx = x$$

Step 4: Formulate General Solution ($y$)

Combine the particular integral $y_p = u\cdot y_1 + v\cdot y_2$ with the complementary system function:

$$y(x) = y_c(x) + y_p(x)$$ $$y(x) = c_1\cos(x) + c_2\sin(x) + \ln|\cos(x)|\cos(x) + x\sin(x)$$
